# CA review — rules folder (FY 2025-26 / AY 2026-27)

Reviewed: 6 July 2026. Scope: all 23 topic files (`.md` + paired `.json`) in `rules/`, every source link, and the substantive tax positions, checked against current CBDT/Income Tax Department material and reliable secondary sources.

The codebase's own honesty is worth noting upfront: most files already carry an explicit `verification.status` and last-checked date, and several correctly admit they're stubs. That self-flagging is doing real work — the two errors below that actually matter are both in files marked "verified," not in the acknowledged stubs.

## 1. Factual errors to fix now

**LRS TCS rate on education/medical remittances is wrong.** `foreign-investments.md` and `.json` state 2% TCS on education (non-loan) and medical remittances above the ₹10 lakh threshold. For FY2025-26 (Budget 2025, effective 1 April 2025) the rate is **5%**, not 2%. The 2% figure is a Budget 2026 proposal, effective only from 1 April 2026 — it doesn't apply to the year this tool is filing for. This is a clean, material error: fix `rate_education_medical` in the JSON from `0.02` to `0.05`, and the corresponding sentence in the `.md`.

**Section 80CCD(2) employer-NPS rate is outdated.** `regime-choice.md`/`.json` and the description in `loan-treatment.json` say the employer-NPS deduction cap is "14% for government employees, 10% for others." Budget 2024 raised the private-sector rate to **14% for everyone** under the new regime, effective FY2024-25 onward — so it's 14% across the board for FY2025-26 under Section 115BAC, not 10% for non-government employees. Worth double-checking whether the old-regime private-sector rate is still 10% (some summaries suggest the 14% enhancement is tied specifically to the new regime) and stating that split explicitly rather than a flat "10% for others."

**Internal date inconsistency on the NRI mutual-fund case.** `nri-dtaa.md` says the *Anushka Sanjay Shah v. ITO* ITAT Mumbai ruling was decided "March 2025." The paired `nri-dtaa.json` gives the date as `"2026-03-26"`. These can't both be right, and they aren't: the correct date, per Indian Kanoon and multiple secondary sources (including a DHC tax alert published weeks later in April 2025), is **26 March 2025**. Fix the JSON. While in there, the app spells the party's name "Anushaka" throughout — the primary record (Indian Kanoon) spells it "**Anushka**." Worth correcting, though this one's cosmetic.

## 2. Correct conclusion, but the reasoning given is incomplete

**Section 87A rebate excluded for equity capital gains — right answer, wrong (or at least incomplete) justification.** `capital-gains-equity.json` states flatly: `"section_87a_rebate_allowed": false`. For FY2025-26 that's now correct, but only because Finance Act 2025 amended the law, effective AY2026-27, to expressly exclude *all* special-rate income — both Section 111A (STCG) and 112A (LTCG) — from the 87A rebate. Before that amendment, this was genuinely contested: CBDT's July 2024 ITR-utility change blocked the rebate against 111A/112A even within the threshold, the Bombay High Court (*Chamber of Tax Consultants v. DGIT(Systems)*, 2025) held that restriction improper for 111A, and ITAT Ahmedabad allowed the rebate against STCG in August 2025. 112A was always excluded by Section 112A(6) itself; 111A was not, until the FY2025-26 amendment closed that gap. A user who read financial-news coverage from mid-2025 (plenty of it says the rebate on STCG *is* available) will be confused by a flat "false" with no explanation. One added sentence — "as amended by Finance Act 2025, effective AY2026-27; before that, 111A rebate eligibility was contested and courts had ruled in the taxpayer's favour" — would close that gap and head off support questions.

## 3. Incomplete rather than wrong

**Section 50AA "specified mutual fund" test is under-specified.** `capital-gains-mutual-funds.json` says specified funds get `classification: short_term_deemed, tax_rate: slab, needs_fund_type_check: true` with no threshold given, and the `.md` is still a stub. The actual test that determines whether a fund falls into this bucket is a **>65% debt/money-market allocation** (the post-Finance-Act-2024 redefinition applicable from FY2025-26). A fund sitting between roughly 35–65% equity doesn't automatically get deemed-STCG treatment — it can still get equity/other-asset treatment. Right now the tool has no way to apply that threshold correctly; this should be prioritised given capital gains on mutual funds is one of the most common income types this audience will have.

**ITR-3 non-audit "31 August" due date is real but narrower than stated.** `itr-form-selection.json` gives a blanket 31 August 2026 due date to all "business_or_speculative_non_audit" filers. That bucket genuinely exists for AY2026-27 (a new departmental change this year — historically non-audit business/professional filers were also due 31 July), but it specifically **excludes** anyone caught by Section 92E (transfer pricing), who instead get 30 November. The tool should carve that exclusion out rather than applying 31 August across the board.

**New Income Tax Act 2025 section-renumbering — mostly solid, three mappings unsettled.** The core framing (Act effective 1 April 2026, FY2025-26/AY2026-27 still governed by the 1961 Act) is confirmed against a PIB press release and holds up throughout the codebase. Also solid: 80C→123, 80D→126, 80E→129, 80EEB→132, 10(10D)→Schedule II Sr. No. 2, the foreign-asset filing requirement continuing under Section 263, and Form 15CA/15CB→145/146 effective 1 April 2026 (including the "form follows filing date, tax treatment follows income year" nuance — that's correctly reasoned). Three specific numbers are contested across secondary sources and shouldn't be stated with the same confidence as the rest:
- Section 208→404, 234B→424, 234C→425 (`advance-tax.md`) — majority-sourced but at least one tracker gives 419/447/448 instead.
- Section 24(b)→22 (`loan-treatment.json`) — one source agrees, another gives 72 and says 22 is actually the old 16(ia) standard-deduction section.
- Section 80EE/80EEA→130 (`loan-treatment.json`) — these look like they may be two separate new sections (e.g. 130 and 131), not both merging into 130.

None of this affects FY2025-26 filing itself since the old numbers govern this year regardless — but if it's shown to users as forward-looking trivia, a soft "pending official CBDT concordance" caveat is more honest than flat certainty. No official CBDT old-to-new section concordance table could be retrieved directly; everything here is triangulated across commentary sites, some of which visibly disagree with each other.

## 4. Source-quality issues (links resolve, but the citation itself is weak)

A few citations are functioning fine but are marketing/SEO content standing in for a claim that deserves a primary source: `getpanda.money` for the USD 1 million NRO repatriation cap (a remittance-app blog; the actual authority is an RBI/FEMA master direction), and Bajaj Finserv for "foreign shares are treated as unlisted shares" (a financial-products company site; the claim itself checked out, just not from this source). `taxsmooth.com` (cited in `loan-treatment.json`) is low-recognition and didn't render at all when fetched — worth swapping for ClearTax or an official source regardless. None of these are wrong, just thinner than they should be for a filing tool.

A handful of official `incometaxindia.gov.in`/`incometax.gov.in` PDFs and pages returned empty content when fetched by an automated tool (Rule 128 text, the Budget 2025 FAQ PDF, the Taxation-of-Non-Resident PDF) — likely a rendering/bot-blocking artefact rather than a dead link, since the substance of each claim was independently corroborated elsewhere, but worth a human opening each one directly to confirm before treating them as checked. The three Economic Times links behind `itr-form-selection`'s due-date claims are blocked to automated fetching entirely; the due-date substance was independently confirmed via ClearTax/Upstox instead, but the citations themselves are unverified.

## 5. Structural gap: stubs that other files lean on

Nine of the 23 topics are still "Stub only"/"PENDING" with no populated `.md` prose: `capital-gains-mutual-funds`, `dividends`, `new-act-2025-transition`, `nri-residential-status`, `senior-citizen-advance-tax-and-regime`, `senior-citizen-basics`, `single-parent-alimony`, and — worth flagging separately — `capital-gains-equity` and `filing-mistakes-and-penalties`, whose **JSON is already populated, sourced, and dated as checked**, while the paired `.md` still says "Stub only." That's a lagging-documentation bug, not a missing-data one: the app may already be computing and displaying correct, verified LTCG/STCG and late-filing figures while the help text a user reads is a placeholder. Worth reconciling those two specifically before the others, since the numbers are already trustworthy.

Two of the true stubs are load-bearing for other files and worth prioritising for that reason: `nri-residential-status.md` is cited by `foreign-investments.md` and implicitly by the ITR-routing logic as the authority on resident/NRI/RNOR status, yet has no day-count test written up at all. `new-act-2025-transition.md` is name-checked by `advance-tax.md`, `insurance.json`, `loan-treatment.json`, and `foreign-investments.md` as "see new-act-2025-transition.md for the same pattern" — but that file itself is empty. Right now those cross-references point at nothing.

`single-parent-alimony` has had no research pass at all yet, by its own admission. For what it's worth, the long-standing judicial position (not something this year's Budget touches) is that periodic maintenance/alimony is taxed as income from other sources in the recipient's hands, while a one-time lump-sum settlement is generally treated as a capital receipt and not taxed — this traces to old Bombay High Court authority on lump-sum alimony. That's a reasonable starting point for the eventual write-up, but it should get the same sourced verification pass as the rest before shipping, not just my say-so here.
